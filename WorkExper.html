Work Experience

Feild trips & Influential Speakers

The majority of my work experience has taught and motivated me to be a respectful, cooperative coworker. Mrs. Sharbach, my eighth grade teacher (2015-2016), taught me and my classmates to fair, respectful people. While Mrs. Sharbach was, officially, my English and History teacher, she was our collective mentor; she pushed us to become better people, chastised us when we were rude, and helped us grow academically. She told us what she thought, why she thought it, and never looked at us as 'just students'. At times, honestly, she did push us too hard but always made sure we were learning and growing as both students and human beings. Mrs. Sharbach is the epitome of an amazing teacher. She encouraged us to give back to our community to anyone and everyone. She forced us to work with people we wouldn't typically work with so we would learn to work with anyone and everyone. She made all of us more flexible, cooperative and respectful people and I can honestly say I would be a complete failure at all three had I not been in her class. As she liked to say she had always taught us to be good people', and I know that today I am both a better person and a better worker because of her.

In 2017, I, along with other students from Dublin High, visited and were shown around the SAP campus in San Ramon. Once there we were divided into 2 groups (one would hear a lecture and the other would be shown around). The first lecture concerned the achievement of success and the second pertained to emotional intelligence, or rather concern for one's coworkers. To me, the lecture on emotional intelligence was particularly important as the ability to respect one's coworkers is an essential but underesti mated skill. After this, we were shown around campus and what struck me most was how absolutely beautiful the campus was and, to me it seemed, how little menial labor was being performed even in the workplace areas. It also struck me how employees were allowed to draw on the walls as a means of communication or play. Overall, the campus appeared to be a collection of tranquil, intelligent people who completely lacked workplace stress. This, from my perspective, seemed to be a result of the employees' cooperative and organized behavior. What I learnt on this field trip was that work doesn't have to be laborious as long as one is willing to cooperate and respect one's coworkers to get work done. To reiterate, from these experiences I have learnt to be respectful, cooperative worker.
