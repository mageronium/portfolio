Entrepreneurship Project

(Project started May 11 2018 Completed May 23 2017, POE Period 7)
The Entrepreneurship Project is a project assigned yearly to all engineering students. It concerns the development of a new solution to solve a widely experienced problem. Depending on the problem, the deliverables for this project could range from a working prototype to a well developed idea for one. My team (team Wake up, Breakfast Robot!) choose to address oversleeping and therefore skipping breakfast or consuming a substandard breakfast. We chose to solve this problem by building and programming a prototype that makes cereal, brings it to the user, wakes the user, and gives them cereal. For this project I was a primary builder. I built the platform, attached the grabbing mechanism, made and attached the multi directional wheels, and assisted in the build of the poking mechanism. I organized the build and came in almost everyday during lunch or Gael to work on the robot. During this project I showed great leadership and perseverance as a primary builder for the project.

Winch Project

(Project 1.2.5, Started- October 16, 2017 Completed- October 20, 2017, POE period 7)
For the 1.2.5 project, or ' Winch Project ', my teammates and I were to build a prototype for a large scale winch that was to be used by local construction company. We were asked to build a winch using a Fisher Tech motor capable of lifting a weight of at least 100 grams, 30 centimeters upward. We were limited to materials found in the classroom and or approved by the teacher. For this project, I was responsible for the building and maintenance of the winch, meaning that I was the one responsible for the winch's ability to pull the weight upward. The first day was spent building the winch's skeleton and the rest of the time was spent improving the winch. During the course of this project, I came in everyday during Gael period to improve and modify the mechanism. This required me to test multiple configurations of the winch and fixing the winch when the mechanism failed. Although I was not the primary builder or developer, I was persistent and unyielding in the optimization of the winch.
